A Novel Design of the Utilisation of Soft Grippers in Loading & Unloading

Soft Robotics

Project details

Description
This research implemented a novel design for a soft gripper for use in loading and unloading applications. This technology allows the gripper to be utilised in assembly and production lines in the factories to deal with the soft, sensitive and fragile objects that are damaged with the use of existing conventional fixed degree of freedom rigid grippers. Also, it aides to simplify the robotic arm control as soft gripper’s does not require setup for each gripped part position. The thesis of this research are based on the half-circular cross section actuator design. Several experiments were conducted to evaluate different configurations and design parameters of the actuator to determine the optimum design for the application. Additionally, a procedure for obtaining a successful casting technique of the actuator material was presented, as well as, some recommendations and observation to design a successful mould to cast the actuators. Using the custom closed loop control system and exquisite design the gripper is able to carry out an object with payload of 1 kilogram relative to its weight of 430 gm. Also, the gripped object can be of any shape, size and with any skin.
