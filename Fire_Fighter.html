Description
Scientific advancements have heightened the responsibility of human protection, necessitating innovative solutions to combat the threat of fires. The emergence of firefighting robots addresses these challenges by employing diverse methods such as water jets, powder, foam fluid, and fire extinguisher balls. These robots are designed to meet market requirements, incorporating multiple effective firefighting techniques for all fire classes, autonomous navigation, stair-climbing capabilities, remote control, and a heat-resistant, easily maintainable body. The robot's outer body is crafted from machined cold-rolled stainless steel for durability, while the internal body utilizes tested fiberglass for its lightweight properties. Acrylic paint provides high temperature resistance. Motion is achieved through four DC motors that transmit torque to shafts, rotating gears meshed with chains. The robot is equipped with tanks for air, foam, and powder, ensuring appropriate materials are used during fire suppression. The control system utilizes Arduino and Raspberry Pi, connected to sensors including proximity IR, gyroscope, air humidity, and temperature sensors. These sensors calculate distances, guide turning maneuvers using gyroscope measurements, and incorporate neural network technology for fire self-tracking through an attached camera. The robot, along with its control unit, is an automated firefighting solution that can be further enhanced for faster and more accurate fire detection. The motion system features a lightweight rubber belt, facilitating smooth operation and minimizing failures by accounting for applied forces on the robot's body.
